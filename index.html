Selected work

Every project starts from the same itch: a legal task that deserves better tooling than copy-paste and blind trust.

01

mcp-legal-it

The problem: large language models are eloquent about the law and frequently wrong about it. The tool: an MCP server that grounds Claude in verified Italian sources — statutes from Normattiva, case law from Italgiure and CeRDEF, EU law from EUR-Lex — plus 200+ deterministic tools for the work lawyers actually bill hours for: interest and revaluation, fee schedules, procedural deadlines, GDPR paperwork. Guardrail hooks verify that every cited norm is backed by a real source lookup.

PythonMCPApache-2.0 CI · versioned releases · 20 skills · 6 agents
02

VisuaLex

The problem: legal research means walls of unstructured text. The tool: a research workbench for Italian and EU sources — an async Python API over Normattiva, EUR-Lex and Brocardi with a React frontend that turns statutes into structured, navigable views. Started as a Colab notebook in 2023; now on its fourth architecture and live in private beta at visualex.org.

PythonTypeScriptReact Live · private beta (access on request)
03

ALIS / MERL-T

The question: can interpretation itself be engineered? An experiment in computational hermeneutics: the letter and the legislator's intent codified in art. 12 of the Italian preleggi, the system around a norm, the case law that applies it — each implemented as an expert in a multi-expert retrieval architecture, aligned through community feedback (RLCF).

PythonFastAPI Research prototype
04

axeScraper

The problem: accessibility compliance is audited one page at a time. The tool: a crawler and axe-core pipeline for large-scale web accessibility audits — the tooling side of WCAG and European Accessibility Act compliance. (This site respects prefers-reduced-motion. Naturally.)

Research

§ Geometria Iuris — measuring legal meaning in embedding spaces.

My master's thesis (LUISS, Methodology of Legal Science) asks whether legal meaning is measurable — treating embedding spaces as the measuring instrument and testing what they can, and cannot, register about legal language across legal traditions. The experimental artefacts are frozen and hash-verified: 50 SHA-256 checksums stand between the results and wishful thinking.

About

I'm a lawyer who writes production code — not a developer who read a law book once. I taught myself engineering because legal work kept running into problems only code could solve properly: sources that need verifying, calculations that need repeating exactly, texts that need structure.

That makes me bilingual in a useful way: I can read a sentenza and a stack trace, draft a GDPR notice and the API that generates it. At SAPG Legal I work on data protection and compliance for technology and AI companies; on my own time I build the open-source tools I wish existed when the workday started.

I build for the Italian legal system first — which is why my projects' documentation is often in Italian. The engineering underneath speaks English, and so do I.
